3 Ways to Boost Creativity

Never Stop Learning

Let’s face it. With the speed at which technology changes, you’re never gonna know everything. The minute you graduate, get the job, or feel like you’ve ‘arrived’… News flash: you haven’t. There’s more value in having the ability and willingness to continue learning and roll with ongoing changes than to settle down lock yourself into “This is how I do my thing, and this is how I’ve always done it. Nothing’s ever gonna change that.” If that’s true for you, you’re gonna get left in the dust. Keep learning.

Push Yourself

It’s great to be genuinely proud of your work. Especially if it’s something that was a result of several days or months of trial and error, trying, failing, and trying again. That’s an awesome feeling, and should totally motivate you every single day. It’s also pretty unrealistic to continuously raise the bar for every single project. You’ll either get burned out or tired of trying to one-up yourself. However, if you set the bar within reason, it’s an awesome tool to continue sharpening your skill set. Definitely continue to strive to be your best and do the best work you can, but every single thing you touch isn’t going to turn to gold. Instead of looking at everyone else around you and thinking, “I’ll never be on their level.” Just look at your own work and think, “Yeah, typically I’d call it finished at this point, but… what would make this better?” And add a little flare. Keep doing that, and you’ll surprise yourself.

You’re Not Perfect

There’s just something about adults and admitting mistakes. No one likes it. But consider the toddlers- when they’re learning to walk and run, they fall all the time. They’re just beginning this whole “walking” thing. They’re inexperienced, but they’re trying. They’re failing here and there, but they don’t stop trying. They don’t fall to the floor, cry and think, “Well, that was a failure. Better quit this and stick to what I know.” They get back up and keep trying, and they don’t care who sees or what anyone else thinks.

Unfortunately, as an adult, that attitude leaves us. Somehow we become embarrassed to make mistakes, and think we should just nail it on the first try. Just stick with it, and it’ll pay off. A lot of this can even be applied to the real world and just life in general, not specifically creativity and design.
